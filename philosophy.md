# The Philosophy of TOI

## 答えは文脈から生まれる

TOI is designed around a simple but radical premise: *meaning emerges from context, not from correct answers.*

Traditional educational tools assume that knowledge is a fixed target. A student who chooses the "right" answer is deemed successful; a student who chooses differently is corrected.

TOI rejects this framing. Each card presents an inquiry without a predetermined answer. The student's selection — whatever it is — becomes part of their personal context, a thread in their unfolding understanding of themselves.

## Triple Etymology

The name *TOI* carries three layered meanings:

- **問い (toi)** — *an inquiry*, the Japanese word for "question"
- **toy** — something playful, something to be handled with curiosity
- **The Original Inquiry** — the first question one asks oneself

Each layer informs the design. TOI is not a quiz. It is not a game in the competitive sense. It is a tool for self-discovery, disguised as a deck of cards.

## Design Principles

1. **No correct answer** — every choice is valid; the context a student builds from their choices is what matters
2. **Same poems, same 100 questions, different goals** — the deck is uniform, but the journey through it is radically individual
3. **Selection as identity** — the pattern of a student's choices over time forms a unique signature
